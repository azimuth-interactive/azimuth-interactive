About Azimuth

Azimuth Interactive is a publisher services company founded in 1985 that provides book publishers with a broad range of services: from strategy consulting to content development, project management, marketing, and traditional production and packaging.

The founders of Azimuth Interactive are Kenneth C. Laudon, Ph.D., author of leading college textbooks and professor at New York University’s Stern School of Business, and Carol Traver, an attorney and co-author of several textbooks and book series.

Azimuth has over twenty years of experience in developing new, innovative products for publishers, primarily in higher education topics, but also in technology training for business, schools, and universities.

What does “Azimuth” mean? “Azimuth” is a navigational term that refers to the direction on Earth of a navigational star. Our company is all about new directions in publishing and learning.

In 1986 Azimuth was one of the first companies in the U.S. to develop software disks and products to accompany college textbooks. In 1995 Azimuth was one of the first companies to develop interactive software training for Microsoft Office products for use in corporate and university training. In 2002 Azimuth developed CD-ROM and Web supplements for leading textbooks used through the world. Azimuth has continued to spearhead the development of interactive products using technologies delivered over the Web, including flash cards, interactive testing, podcasts, and video.

While we began our company in the cauldron of new learning technologies in the late 1990s and during the dot.com era, we were never just a learning technology company. We are also content creators and authors of several leading textbooks used throughout the world (“E-commerce. Business. Technology. Society,” (Prentice Hall) and “Management Information Systems: Managing the Digital Firm” (Prentice Hall).

Based on our experience creating content, we expanded to helping publishers manage the development of content for entire series of books along with related digital content. Identifying and vetting authors, Azimuth now provides publishers with a highly responsive network of authors that handle just about any content creation project. Azimuth provides complete start-to-finish project management services, reducing costs for publishers and delivering products on time and on budget.

As budgets for marketing become strained, we have helped publishers develop unique Web-based marketing strategies to reach the new kinds of students and readers.

Azimuth has moved beyond project management to include production services—designing layouts and compositing. We have developed a new model of production which greatly compresses the time needed to produce complex books and entire book series.
